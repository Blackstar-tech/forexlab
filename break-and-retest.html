Trade the pullback after a key level break. High probability, low risk entries.

What Is the Break and Retest Strategy?

The break and retest strategy is one of the most reliable entry techniques in Forex trading. When price breaks above a resistance level (or below support), it often comes back ("retests") that level before continuing in the breakout direction. Professional traders wait for the retest, then enter when price holds the level. This gives you an ultra-low-risk entry with a tight stop loss. The strategy has high win rates because you're entering on a failed attempt by bears/bulls to push back below/above the broken level.

Example 4: Why Retests Create High-Probability Trades

The Institution's Perspective - Why Retest = Opportunity

✔ Break and Retest Trading Rules

Rule 1: Identify the Resistance/Support Level

Look for a level price has tested 2-3 times without breaking. This is where you expect the break.

Rule 2: Wait for the CLEAR BREAK

Price must break ABOVE resistance (for long) or BELOW support (for short) by at least 5-10 pips. This confirms the break is real, not a false spike.

Rule 3: Wait for the RETEST

DON'T enter on the break. Wait for price to come back and retest the broken level. This is your entry zone (±5 pips from the level).

Rule 4: Check if Price HOLDS the Retest

When price retests, it should bounce off or consolidate. If it immediately breaks back below the level, this is a false break—SKIP the trade.

Rule 5: Place Stop Loss Tight

Your stop goes just below the retest level (for longs) or above it (for shorts). Maximum 10-15 pips. Keep losses small.

Rule 6: Set Target at Next Resistance/Support

Your target is the next major level in the direction of the break. Aim for 1:2.5 risk-to-reward minimum.

Rule 7: Accept False Breaks as Part of the Strategy

30% of your trades will be false breaks. Your tight stops mean you lose only -$100. Your winners gain +$250. The math is in your favor.
