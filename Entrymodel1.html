The Exact Rules

How The FX Lab
Model Works

01
Wait For Liquidity To Be Raided

The model begins when price sweeps a key liquidity level — equal highs, equal lows, or swing points where stop losses are clustered. This raid is the market hunting liquidity before it makes its true move. Do not enter during the raid. The raid is not your signal — it is your warning that the signal is coming.

Equal Highs Swept Equal Lows Swept Swing High/Low Taken
↓
02
Confirm Displacement To The Opposite Side

After the raid, watch for a strong, impulsive candle (or series of candles) that moves aggressively in the opposite direction — this is displacement. It signals that institutions have reversed their position and price is now being driven with intent. Weak moves do not qualify. The displacement must be sharp, clean, and decisive, leaving Fair Value Gaps (FVGs) in its wake.

Impulsive Rejection Candle Strong Momentum Shift FVGs Created
↓
03
Enter At The Second FVG

Displacement creates multiple Fair Value Gaps. The FX Lab Model specifies entry at the second FVG — not the first. The first FVG is often too aggressive and price may pierce through it. The second FVG provides a more refined, higher-probability entry as price returns to fill inefficiency before continuing in the direction of the displacement. Wait for price to retrace back into this second FVG and look for a confirmation candle before executing.
